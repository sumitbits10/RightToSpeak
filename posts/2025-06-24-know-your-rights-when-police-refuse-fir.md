Filing a First Information Report (FIR) is your legal right. The police **cannot refuse** to register an FIR in cognizable offences. Yet many citizens in India face this situation daily.

## What Is a Cognizable Offence?

A cognizable offence is one where police can arrest without a warrant — murder, theft, rape, kidnapping, dacoity etc. For these, registration of FIR is **mandatory** under Section 154 of CrPC (now Section 173 of BNSS 2023).

## What To Do If Police Refuse

### Step 1 — Demand a written refusal
Ask the officer in charge to give you the reason for refusal in writing. Most officers will back down at this step alone.

### Step 2 — Send FIR by post to the SP
Under Section 154(3) CrPC, you can send your FIR complaint in writing **by post** directly to the Superintendent of Police. The SP must investigate or direct registration.

### Step 3 — File a complaint before the Magistrate
Under Section 156(3) CrPC, a Judicial Magistrate can order the police to register an FIR and investigate. This is a powerful tool.

### Step 4 — File a writ petition in High Court
If all else fails, approach the High Court with a writ petition. Courts take police inaction very seriously and have repeatedly held that refusal to file FIR is a violation of fundamental rights.

## Important Points To Remember

- Keep a copy of everything you submit to police.
- Note down the date, time, name and badge number of the officer who refused.
- Take a witness along if possible.
- You can also call **Police Helpline 100** and register a complaint.
